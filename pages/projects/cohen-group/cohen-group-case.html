Case: Cohen Group Northwest

The Cohen Group is the next generation of Real Estate committed to communities. They fundamentally believe good business and doing good for the community go hand in hand. The Cohen Group associates have been in the industry since 2012 and in 2017 decided that they could provide superior services and experiences as a team. Whether it is finding a home for a new family or an investor, the Cohen Group drives from the core belief that we are neighbors first . They diligently inform, guide and facilitate the best solution for all.

Anything created is more potent when the ideal it is derived from and the environment in which it functions are in alignment.

Compass and Constitutiont

Need

Give body to the Cohen Groups core values through a brand identity that unmistakenly conveyed their driving force.

Solution

Our first step was to distill the guiding principles that brought the team together into a compass that would guide our design, marketing, and business decisions.

We believe in building wealth in families and communities beyond the monetary. Above all, we are founded on partnerships based on aligned values. Each relationship is promised integrity, diligence and personalized attention. To drive these ideals we are committed to built business practices driven by innovation, education, and collaboration throughout each and every relationship we touch.
- Excerpt from Cohen Group Brand Compass

Mark

Need

The Cohen Group is a Northwest brand with a down to earth mentality and dash of idealism. Accompanied by the nature of a Real Estate firm, which is to invest in the value of an area and augmenting natural wealth with developments that foster healthy living.

Solution

We knew we were making a commitment and investment into the Northwest. We couldn't be more explicit than evergreen trees and a home built under the mountains. This created an at a glance recognizable Northwest real estate mark. Using clean and simple lines with modern geometry, touching on professional precision. We stayed true to our primary need to be recognizable and legibility even in the mist and downpours of the Northwest.

Typography

Need

We needed a modern typeface that could be read from near and far and looked great in print and on the web.

Solution

We chose Lato; a clean open source, web safe font that looks great on every median the industry required. Our primary goal was to deliver information to our clients and community in a way that quickly informs allowing for intelligent decisions on their futures. The Lato family ensures that we meet all the accessibility needs of our audiences without sacrificing an aesthetically pleasing experience.

Color

Need

Being a Keller Willaims Team we had to use the trademark red. We needed a palette that could work well with the red but was uniquely Cohen Group.

Solution

Our core values of a grounded, straightforward approach led us to a refined off-black, and off-white compliments with a stable naval blue. A simple classic color palette driving from our desire to deliver consistent quality and strong investment opportunities.

Cards

Need

The Real Estate business is centered around creating relationships and delivering on the promise that you have a family's or investors best interest at heart. We needed business cards that said as much and more.

Solution

To meet this need we designed cards that make impressions. Full bleed photographs so clients know who they are working with. Clear hierarchy of elements so the important elements are there at a glance. A paper weight that lets people know, if we take half as much care with business relationships, as with our business cards they are in good hands. For a quality of print, we knew just where to go and we put our job in the incredible hands of Moo.

Signage

Need

Next on the agenda were glorious signs. We needed something our clients could recognize even in the worst Northwest weather. We took a survey of sign design on an international scale. Scouring the internet for legibility methodologies nation to nation.

Solution

We kept a no-nonsense approach with a strong San Serif, complimented with good old fashion typographic hierarchy and decent white space so the most important information could be jotted down at 50 miles an hour in a down poor. We drove for high contract and a clear realationships between our elements so when our sign loomed out of the rain they knew who they were working with.
